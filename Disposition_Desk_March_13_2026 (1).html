Lead Story

Foreclosure Activity Rises for the 12th Straight Month - REO Completions Up 35% Year-Over-Year

ATTOM's February 2026 Foreclosure Market Report, released Thursday, March 12, confirms that foreclosure activity is accelerating into spring. With 38,840 total filings last month and REO completions running 35% above year-ago levels, the pipeline of bank-owned properties continues to grow. Foreclosure starts rose 14% year-over-year to 25,928 properties, signaling more REO inventory coming to market in the months ahead. For UFS agents, this remains a sustained window of REO opportunity across major markets.

Section 4 Industry News and Market Context
ATTOM Data Solutions · March 12, 2026

February 2026 Foreclosure Market Report: Gradual Annual Rise Continues for 12th Straight Month

ATTOM confirmed that 38,840 U.S. properties had foreclosure filings in February, down 4% from January but up 20% from a year ago. REO completions reached 4,077, up 35% year-over-year. CEO Rob Barber noted that while filings dipped slightly month-over-month, both foreclosure starts and completed foreclosures remain higher than a year ago and still well below historic norms.

ATTOM Data Solutions · February 11, 2026

January 2026: Foreclosure Starts Up 26%, REO Completions Jump 59% Year-Over-Year

January recorded 40,534 total filings, up 32% year-over-year, with REO completions surging 59% above year-ago levels to 4,714. Texas led all states with 573 REOs. The January data confirmed the accelerating pace of the foreclosure cycle entering 2026.

ATTOM Data Solutions · February 19, 2026

Q1 2026 Zombie Foreclosure Report: Vacancy Holds Steady at 1.3% - Zombie Count Drops in 28 States

ATTOM's first-quarter 2026 vacant property report showed the vacant residential property rate held steady at 1.3%, while zombie foreclosures declined in 28 states and D.C. That suggests the foreclosure pipeline is being processed rather than allowed to stall in limbo.

Market Context · Week of March 9, 2026

Indiana Emerges as a Major Foreclosure Flashpoint

Indiana climbed into the top five foreclosure states in February with 1,197 foreclosure starts, while Indianapolis and Evansville both broke into the national top five for worst filing rates. Michigan also rose to the number two spot nationally for REO completions with 432 repos.

Market Context · Week of March 9, 2026

Florida REO Remains Dominant

Lakeland, Punta Gorda, and other Florida markets continue to hold multiple spots among the worst metro filing rates. Florida ranked #2 nationally for foreclosure starts in February with 3,250 new filings and #3 for bank repos with 364 REO completions.
