01 — Brand Foundation

The go-to national field services platform — where every order is tracked, every property is protected, and every client trusts the outcome.

Professional. Reliable. Accountable. Every field service delivered on time, on spec, and fully documented.

Brand Personality

Dependable Operator We don't over-promise. We deliver. Our tone is direct, confident, and grounded — like a 15-year veteran who's seen everything and has a system for all of it.

Brand Values

  • → Reliability over speed
  • → Transparency in process
  • → Professionalism in every interaction
  • → Industry expertise, not just service

Positioning

For REO professionals who need a field services partner they can trust at scale — UFS is the one call that handles everything.

02 — Logo System

Logo & Mark

The UFS identity is built on a hexagonal mark — a symbol of structure, reliability, and precision — paired with our wordmark in Playfair Display.

On Black — Primary
On White — Secondary
On Red — Special Use
On Gray — Alternate

✓ Do

Use only approved color combinations. Maintain clear space equal to the height of the "U" around all edges. Scale proportionally only. Use vector files for all print.

✗ Don't

Don't stretch, skew, or rotate the logo. Don't add drop shadows or effects. Don't use on complex photo backgrounds without a clear protective field. Don't recreate the logo in other typefaces.

Minimum Size

Digital: 120px wide minimum
Print: 30mm wide minimum

Below these sizes, use the standalone hex mark only.

Clear Space

The minimum clear space around the logo equals the height of the letter "U" in the wordmark. No other elements may enter this zone — text, imagery, or other logos.

05 — Voice & Tone

How We Speak

UFS speaks like a trusted industry veteran — confident, direct, never salesy. We lead with competence, not hype.

Direct

We say what we mean without filler. No buzzwords, no fluff. Every sentence earns its place.

"Submit your order. We handle the rest."

Expert

We speak the language of REO. We understand the workflow. We reference real job types, real platforms, real expectations.

"Occupancy checks, preservation orders, lock changes — all tracked in one place."

Warm

Professional doesn't mean cold. We're human. We build relationships. We follow up.

"Really enjoyed connecting with you today."

✓ Write This

Submit your first order in 60 seconds.
We handle inspections, occupancy checks, and preservation.
Your documentation is ready when your asset manager asks for it.
Track every order in real time from your portal.
No forms to fill out — I can set your account up right now.

✗ Not This

Leverage our best-in-class synergistic solutions.
We are a full-service provider of comprehensive residential real estate maintenance.
Please do not hesitate to reach out at your earliest convenience.
Revolutionize your real estate business with our streamlined platform!
We are thrilled to announce our exciting new partnership.

Email Tone

Conversational and action-oriented. Short paragraphs. One clear CTA per email. Never more than 3 paragraphs before the ask.

Website Tone

Confident and benefit-led. Lead with what the client gets. Use active voice. Sub-headlines that answer "why should I care?"

Newsletter Tone

Industry-expert voice. Data-first. Insightful, not promotional. The Disposition Desk informs — it doesn't sell. One soft CTA per issue, at the end.

06 — Iconography

Icons & Symbols

Icons reinforce service categories. Use outline-style, 2px stroke weight. Never use filled icons in primary UI — reserve fills for status indicators only.

✓ Icon Usage Rules

Use 24×24px grid. 1.5px stroke weight. Rounded line-caps. Always use the same icon style library throughout a single document or template (Lucide preferred).

✗ Icon Misuse

Don't mix filled and outline icon styles. Don't resize icons non-uniformly. Don't use icons smaller than 16px in UI. Don't use stock emoji as substitute icons in professional materials.

08 — UI Components

Buttons & Controls

Button hierarchy is strict. One primary action per view. Secondary actions are subordinate — never competing visually with the primary.

Primary CTASecondary ActionOutline DarkOutline RedDisabled

Button Rules

Min touch target: 44×44px. Use "→" arrow on primary CTAs. Border-radius: 2px (sharp, professional). Letter-spacing: 0.04em. Font-weight: 600. No rounded "pill" buttons in professional contexts.

Email Buttons

Always use HTML table-based buttons for email (not CSS-only). Background-color on the td element. Padding: 14px 28px. Width: auto, not full-width unless mobile. Always include a fallback text link below.

09 — Photography & Imagery

Visual Language

Photography communicates competence and scale. Use real properties, real work, real outcomes — not stock smiles or abstract tech metaphors.

Exterior property photos,
work-in-progress shots

✓ UseExterior property photography — before/after preservation. Aerial shots of REO portfolios. Field reps on-site. Actual work order documentation photography.

Generic office stock,
smiling realtors

✗ AvoidGeneric stock photography, smiling people in suits, abstract tech imagery, cityscapes that don't relate to our service areas, staged "professional" photos that feel inauthentic.

Treatment

Slightly desaturated with high contrast. Dark overlay (30–40% black) when used as hero backgrounds. Never filtered with vintage/warm tones.

Aspect Ratios

Hero: 16:9. Card thumbnails: 4:3 or 3:2. Email header: 600×200px. Social: 1:1 or 4:5 for Instagram, 1.91:1 for LinkedIn.

File Formats

Web: WebP with JPEG fallback. Email: JPEG only (universal support). Print: TIFF or EPS, 300dpi minimum. Never GIF for photography.

10 — Digital Applications

Email & Digital Standards

Email is our primary outreach channel. Every email must render on dark-mode clients, mobile screens, and legacy Outlook. Table-based HTML required.

Email Specs

  • Max width: 600px
  • Background: #F7F5F2
  • Content bg: #FFFFFF
  • Font stack: DM Sans, then system
  • Body text: 16px / 1.6 line-height
  • No web fonts in Outlook

Subject Line Rules

  • Max 50 characters
  • Title case, no ALL CAPS
  • No emoji in cold outreach
  • Lead with benefit or context
  • Preheader: 85–100 chars
  • A/B test cold subject lines

Accessibility

  • Min contrast ratio: 4.5:1
  • All images need alt text
  • Link text must be descriptive
  • Min font size: 14px in email
  • Touch targets: 44×44px min
  • Test with VoiceOver / NVDA
